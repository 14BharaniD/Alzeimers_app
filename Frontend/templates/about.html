Project Overview

Our multimodal framework combines cutting-edge deep learning techniques with comprehensive data analysis for early Alzheimer's detection

Innovative Approach to Early Diagnosis

Traditional Alzheimer's diagnosis relies on cognitive tests and brain imaging, which often detect the disease at later stages. Our multimodal framework enables earlier detection by analyzing subtle changes in handwriting and speech patterns.

Non-invasive assessment - No medical procedures required
Remote monitoring - Accessible from anywhere
Continuous tracking - Monitor changes over time

By combining handwriting analysis with speech recognition, our system achieves higher accuracy than single-modality approaches, providing a comprehensive assessment of cognitive function.

Dual Modality Analysis

Our system leverages two complementary data sources for comprehensive assessment

Handwriting Analysis

We extract 450+ numerical features from digital handwriting samples, capturing subtle changes in motor control and cognitive function.

Key Features Extracted:

  • Pressure dynamics and stroke velocity
  • Tremor analysis and micrographia detection
  • Spatial organization and letter formation
  • Pause duration and writing rhythm
  • Pen lifts and connection patterns

Speech Recognition & Analysis

We analyze Mel-frequency cepstral coefficients (MFCC) from voice recordings to identify speech patterns associated with cognitive decline.

Speech Characteristics Analyzed:

  • Phonetic changes and articulation rate
  • Hesitation patterns and word-finding difficulties
  • Prosody and intonation variations
  • Speech fluency and pause patterns
  • Semantic content and verbal memory
